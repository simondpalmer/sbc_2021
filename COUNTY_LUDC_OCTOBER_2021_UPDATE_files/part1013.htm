A. Site Design.

1. Berming/Bunkering.

a. If ground equipment cannot be screened by natural topography and/or existing vegetation, and the provision of new landscape screening is not considered feasible or appropriate, the equipment areas may be screened through the use of bunkers, low berms, or a combination of both.

b. Bunker walls should not be visible from public viewing areas.

c. Low berms (two to three) may be used on sides of the facility visible from public roadways. The berms should be naturally contoured using excess cut material from the site.

d. Any berming and/or bunkering should avoid impacts to existing vegetation and should not create additional erosion problems.

e. The antennas should be located out of the skyline as much as possible (move downslope).
